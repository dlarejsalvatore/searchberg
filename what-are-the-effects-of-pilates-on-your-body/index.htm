What Are the Effects of Pilates On Your Body?

While most of us believe Pilates to be synonymous with toned and lean bodies, even those who are new to this form of workout will know that the benefits exceed just that. Pilates is excellent for building core strength and flexibility and if your goal is to achieve a certain desired body type, it should work wonders for you.

How does Pilates benefit you?

But there are other benefits to it as well such as boosting the heart rate, improving bone density and as a total-body workout it can also help you ease back pain. If you’re looking to switch up your usual weight lifting and cardio routine, and want to take your fitness a step further to see groundbreaking results, Pilates can be of great use to you.

Reduce the risk of injury

Pilate’s workouts focus on stretching and balancing which inadvertently help improves flexibility and balance and helps increase strength in the muscles. Greater flexibility reduces the risk of injury because your muscles are more likely to be able to adapt to a wider range of movement.

With improved balance, you’re less likely to lose your balance and fall. Your overall awareness of your own body tends to increase which reduces stress on your tendons, muscles and ligaments.

 

Improve your posture 

Pilates is excellent for reversing the muscular imbalances caused by bad habits such as constantly hunching over. Most of us tend to sit at a desk all day in not so ergonomic chairs or we spend most of our time looking down and scrolling on our smart phones, which strains the neck. This leads to poor posture and hunched shoulders and looks bad! With Pilates, you can work on the weaker muscles in your body and on developing a strong core.

Build cardiovascular endurance

Higher intensity forms of Pilates require you to move from exercise to exercise fairly quickly which gets the heart rate going. Reducing the resting time and incorporating more advanced moves like side lifts, jackknife and planks can boost the heart rate significantly in the target zone.

Strengthen the core

One of the best ways to strengthen your abdominal muscles and target your oblique muscles more effectively than crunches is through Pilates. The exercises require you to contract your abdominal muscles to keep the spine intact which stresses on correct alignment and proper form.
