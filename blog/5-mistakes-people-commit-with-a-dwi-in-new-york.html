5 Mistakes People Commit with a DWI in New York

Driving While Intoxicated (DWI) arrests are common in the state of New York. With alcohol involvement cited as the most common contributing factor to the thousands of motor accidents in the state, it’s no wonder that DWIs are frequent in the state of 19,453,561 people.

While you don’t set out to drive under the influence with the intention to cause any harm to yourself or others, mistakes happen. Sometimes, this fleeting lapse in judgment can leave you with a lifelong criminal record.

There are some mistakes you can make after your DWI charge that can hurt your case considerably. These are some of the common mistakes people make after a DWI arrest in NY:

Admitting To Driving While Intoxicated

Driving while under the influence of drugs, alcohol or other intoxicants can impair your reflexes and alter how you perceive your surroundings. If your blood alcohol content (BAC) is .08 or higher, you’ll pass the designated BAC level to qualify as intoxicated.

Some people think it’ll help their case if they admit to having a beer or glass of wine. Admitting to indulging in alcohol before getting behind the wheel does more harm than good. You have a constitutional right against self-incrimination, it’s important to use it and refrain from providing any information that will be used against you.

Questioning The Authority

You may feel cornered when you’re stopped for driving while intoxicated but it’s important to maintain your composure. Any disrespect toward the law enforcement officers will only work against your case.

It’s important to remember that law enforcement officers have a right to arrest you and arguing against their authority will only increase your chances of getting arrested.

Refusing To Take The Breath Test

In order to charge you with a DWI, law enforcement officers have to prove your BAC. Refusing to take the breathalyzer doesn’t just result in license revocation and fine, it also seems more incriminating. In some cases, a refusal to take the breathalyzer will result in an additional ticket.

Assuming You’re Guilty

Getting arrested for a DWI doesn’t guarantee that you’ll be convicted for the charges. Charges and convictions differ and, thus, your behavior after getting arrested shouldn’t ruin your chances of getting your charges reduced or dismissed.
