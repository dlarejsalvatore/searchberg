The Right Way to Sharpen Saw Blades

Posted bydndwoodworksNovember 9, 2019Posted inSaw Blades

Table saws that are used regularly can wear down and become too dull to use. People who aren’t familiar with woodworking tend to buy a new saw right away—but that’s not needed. Instead of buying a saw, invest in a great set of blades. By replacing the blades of your saw, you get all the benefits of a new saw at the fraction of the cost.

Here are the steps to sharpen saw blades the right way:

1. Replace the Circular Blades with a Diamond Blade

To sharpen a table saw blade, you’ll need to invest in a diamond blade. Before you replace the blade make sure the saw is switched off and unplugged.

2. Take Safety Measures

Before proceeding to the next step, you’re going to need to put on a pair of strong safety goggles, a pair of gloves and a pair of earphones to minimize noise from the machine.

You’re working with sharp pieces of metal which is why you need high-quality gloves—any sort of clumsiness when working with saws can get really messy!

If you’re up for it, you may want to invest in respirators to protect you from dust.

To prop up the blade, you can use a jig to prevent it from damaging the table you’re working on; drywall will work too, but a jig is a lot easier.

3. Clean the Inside of the Blade

Place the circular blade you’re sharpening next to the diamond blade. Since all the carbide is on the inside of the tooth, this is the area that has to be sharpened. Sharpen the inside of the tooth using the diamond blade.

It’ll take some time but you’ll get the hang of it.

4. Get Sharpening

Switch on your table saw and slowly push the first tooth against the diamond blade. Touch it for a short time before pulling it back. Once you’re done with the first one, move on the next. Continue doing this until all blades have been sharpened.

Bear in mind that the above technique doesn’t work on all blades. When you’re working with a really old table saw that has a curved blade instead of a straight edge, you’ll need to find a modern version of the blade.

Also, when blades run out of carbide, they can’t be sharpened anymore and will need to be replaced.
