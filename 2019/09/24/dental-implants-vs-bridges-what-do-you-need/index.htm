Dental Implants vs. Bridges: What Do You Need?

Tooth loss is more common than you thought. Partial and complete tooth loss among adults has increased since the 1970s, according to a survey done by the National Health and Nutrition Association. On average, adults have 24.92 remaining teeth according to this survey.

While it can be quite embarrassing, tooth loss is no joke. Losing one’s teeth could be a sign of a more critical health condition like gum disease, rheumatoid arthritis, diabetes, etc.

There are two main treatment options for those suffering from tooth loss, namely dental implants and bridges, but which option suits you best? The dentists at Rapha Dental are here to help you out:

What are dental implants?

Dental implants are a more permanent solution to tackling tooth loss. A titanium post is bonded into the jaw to support the crown. The titanium root is fused into the jaw surgically.

The process

The first part of the entire process is removing any roots of the previous tooth that might still be present to make way for the titanium post. The titanium screw is then surgically inserted into the jaw and over a period of 3 to 6 months, it fuses into the jawbone. An abutment is attached to the post frame. The dental implant is prepared and kept ready to be fixed into the titanium post.

Types of implants

There are two main kinds of dental implants: endosteal implants and subperiosteal implants.

 Endosteal are titanium posts fused into the jawbone during surgery after which a second surgery is performed to connect the post to the implant once the gum heals.

Subperiosteal implants, on the other hand, are metal frames fitted onto the jawbone below the gum tissue. As the gums heal, the frame fused into the jawbone.

Who should get dental implants?

Dental implants are great for those who are looking to have a long-term solution to tooth loss. The crowns a lifetime and there’s little maintenance required. This dental solution is also ideal for those who don’t want to put additional pressure on their other teeth.

What are dental bridges?

Dental bridges have been developed to reduce the gap between one or more missing teeth.  Dental bridges use other existing teeth to support the false teeth. Dental bridges comprise two or more crowns, depending on the need of each patient.

Types of dental bridges

There are three main kinds of bridges available today:

Traditional bridges are one of the most common bridges used today. They are made of porcelain or ceramics. Traditional bridges are created by a crown for the tooth on each side to provide support.

Cantilever bridges are less common and only appropriate for use for the front teeth. Since it can’t sustain much force, it cannot be placed at the back of the mouth. These bridges can only be used when one side of the gap is missing.

Maryland bonded bridges are made of porcelain. The existing teeth on either sides of the gap are bonded together with the wings on the bridge.

Who should get dental bridges?

Getting dental bridges is a simple procedure that is much quicker than dental implants. They’re great for those looking for a quick yet cheap solution for tooth loss.
