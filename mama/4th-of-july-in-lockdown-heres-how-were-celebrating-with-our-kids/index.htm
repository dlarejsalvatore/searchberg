    6 Covid-safe Ways to Celebrate 4th of July

    Looking for ways to make Fourth of July feel special in lockdown? In this article, we share our favorite recipe ideas, activities and Independence Day inspo!

    Summer Land Author + Writer

    While many of us are used to catching up with friends for town fireworks, live music and red, white & blue popsicles, it’s safe to say that July 4th, 2020 celebrations are going to be slightly different this year.

    Depending on where you live and what level of social distancing restrictions you’re on, there’s a good chance you might need to make your Independence Day celebration a small family affair.

    If you’re looking for recipe ideas, activities, and other inspo, scroll down to see how we’re celebrating with our kids:

     

    #1 Family Breakfast in PJs

     

    Since the 4th is on a Saturday this year, we’re thinking about having a long lazy weekend breakfast at home. Some of our best conversations happen at breakfast and this is a fantastic time to talk about the meaning of the holiday. (Side note – if you’re looking for child-behavior tips, you need this adorable and wise manners book from Marie-Chantal called Manners Begin at Breakfast!)

    Admittedly obsessed with family-matching PJs, we could not be more excited about the Americana Popsicle PJs from HART + LAND. All made from insanely soft pima cotton, they feature original and exclusive prints of red, white & blue popsicles.

    If you happen to have a pool or access to a lake or ocean, why not cool down with a quick dip? Can’t find anywhere to swim? Don’t fret – throw the sprinklers on and let the kids run wild!     #4 Lunch & BBQ Ideas

     

    Nothing says Independence like chowing into some good old fashioned Americana burgers, hot dogs and chips! If you’re getting a little stir-crazy, try packing a cheeseboard picnic and walking to the park. Check out these patriotic recipe ideas on Pinterest.

     

     

    #5 Down time before fireworks!

     

    If you’re like us – in constant need of a nap, then you may want to plan a late afternoon activity for your tots. Try something calming and fun like non-toxic play-dough or an activity pack!
