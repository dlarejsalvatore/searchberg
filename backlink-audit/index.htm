Frequently Asked Questions

A backlink audit is the process of analyzing your current backlinks (also known as your backlink profile). This allows you to correct potential issues (such as 404 errors and redirecting issues) and improve optimization strategies to strengthen your backlink profile.

As bad links are identified and link building strategies are reworked, your website will become better equipped to climb SERPs (search engine result pages) and attract quality traffic.    

A comprehensive backlink audit is a necessity for businesses in 2020. It allows you to get a bird’s-eye view of your website’s backlink profile and make adjustments to rank higher. In addition, you can detect whether your website is at risk of being penalized and take measures to nip potential damage in the bud.

A bad/toxic backlink is an unnatural or low-quality link from an ill-reputed or untrusted site. If left unremoved, bad links can harm your website’s search rankings and reduce the quality and quantity of traffic.

Bad backlinks are commonly received from:

  • Spam websites
  • Virus-infected websites
  • Link networks
  • Websites that have not been indexed by Google

Yes, bad backlinks can reduce your visibility on the web by hurting your rankings. Websites that offer bad backlinks have poor search engine visibility. If your website’s on the receiving end of a bad backlink, it’ll substantially drop on SERPs (search engine result pages). This means less quality traffic and a deteriorating brand identity by virtue of association with an untrusted website.

A high-quality backlink is a link that comes from a website with high domain authority, i.e. an impressive ranking on SERPs (search engine result pages). In short, it’s a gold mine for businesses. Not only will you attract a plethora of quality traffic, but you’ll also boost your rankings and become an authoritative voice in your niche.
