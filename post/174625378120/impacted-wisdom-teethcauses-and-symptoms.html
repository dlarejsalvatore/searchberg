Impacted Wisdom Teeth—Causes And Symptoms

Wisdom teeth refer to the third molars, located at the back of your teeth. Usually they start appearing between the ages of seventeen and twenty-one. However, there are times when there is no space available in your mouth for the teeth to emerge.

These kinds of teeth are known as wisdom teeth. In some cases, these types of teeth might emerge sideways or might be tilted.

How An Impacted Wisdom Tooth Can Affect You?

Impacted wisdom teeth do not always cause pain. In some cases, people do not even realize that they have an impacted wisdom tooth. However, when these teeth start emerging they might cause the flap on top of the gums to swell and get infected.

The swelling can cause pain, which can spread to your ears, neck, and jaw. It can also lead to a condition known as Pericoronitis. In such cases, you even require surgery.

Another problem that you can face is that impacted wisdom tooth can develop cavities. The impacted tooth can also affect the nearby teeth.

It can move them away and result in tooth decay and diseases in the gums. In some case, an impacted wisdom tooth can also lead to other growths in the jaw.  It creates a sac in your mouth, which fills up with fluids overtime.

The sac develops into Cyst overtime and damages the jawbone and the nearby nerves. In case of extensive damage, you might need to get the tissue or the bone removed.

What Are The Symptoms Of An Impacted Wisdom Tooth?

Some of the signs of an impacted wisdom tooth include:

  • Problems in opening the jaw
  • You have a bad taste in your mouth
  • Your breath smells bad
  • You feel pain when you chew or eat your food

Is It Possible To Prevent This Problem?

There is no way in which you can prevent this problem from occurring. However, what you can do is regularly visit the dentist in order to keep track of the growth of the tooth.

Treatment

In order to alleviate the pain, you can rinse your mouth with saltwater. If the pain persists, you have the option of going for tooth extraction. The process of extracting the tooth can last for five to thirty minutes.

Sometimes, in case of an infection you might even need to take antibiotics. After the removal of the tooth, your jaw and cheeks might swell, and it would be a little hard for you to eat certain kinds of food for some time.

What Happens When You Ignore Dental Cavities

Dental cavities are the damaged parts or holes present in the tooth enamel, the outer hard surface of the teeth.

The plaque that feeds on the food that is hanging on your teeth tends to bore down on the tooth enamel, thereby weakening it. Therefore, if you don’t have the habit of brushing and flossing your teeth, then there are high chances that you will develop dental cavities.

Most cavities surface in the most uncomfortable ways possible; however, many have the ability to grow under the radar. But this doesn’t mean that you can choose to ignore them. Dental cavities happen because of tooth decay, which is why ignoring has severe consequences.

Your teeth may go through the following stages if you choose to ignore tooth decay:

White Spots

The first stage of tooth decay is the formation of yellowish/white spots and chalky areas on the tooth enamel. This generally happens when the teeth start losing calcium.

Since this is an early stage, it is possible to reverse it with a preventive measures and mild treatments.

Enamel Breakdown

The second stage is the breakdown of enamel in the teeth. This happens very slowly, as the enamel first breaks down under the surface layer, while the surface remains intact.

If the teeth are left untreated, the enamel then starts to breakdown from the surface layer too, which is irreversible.

Dentin Breakdown

When the dentin below the tooth enamel starts to break down, it results in extreme pain and discomfort. This should be treated as a dental emergency and can be reversed with tooth filing.

Pulp Infection

The pulp refers to the blood vessels and nerves of the teeth. When the tooth decay reaches the pulp, it form pus and start affecting the nerves in your teeth.

Root canal is the only viable treatment for this situation.

Abscess Formation

This is final stage of tooth decay, where the tip of the root, as well as the surrounding bones, starts to decay. It could also lead to swelling in the cheeks.

Treatment for this usually includes painkillers and antibiotics to deal with the infection. Depending on the condition of the teeth, the dentist may perform a root canal, or may have to remove the decaying teeth altogether.

In several teeth are removed, you will need to get partial dentures to replace the missing teeth.

How to prevent Dental Cavities in the Future?

There are no shortcuts to proper dental hygiene.

The best way to prevent dental cavities in the future is to brush and floss your teeth twice a day. In addition to that, schedule an appointment with your dentist twice a year for thorough cleaning.

Common Teeth Whitening Mistakes To Avoid And Why It’s Important To Get It Done Professionally

Everyone wants a nice smile.

However, that is not possible if you do not take care of your teeth. Your lifestyle choices, like the food you eat, as well as your age have an effect on the appearance of your teeth.

In order to improve the appearance of their teeth, people often undergo a process known as teeth whitening. However, this process is completely ineffective if you make the following mistakes:

Bleaching The Teeth

There are people who often assume that teeth-whitening merely entails the whitening of teeth. In order to change the appearance of their teeth they use products like bleach. However, it can do more harm than good.

Not only does bleach affect your teeth, it can also damage your gums. Even if you do indeed manage to whiten your teeth, without making a few lifestyle changes, you will not be able to ensure that your teeth stay white.

Some of the ways in which you can retain your teeth’s appearance include:

· Take good care of your teeth. Brush them multiple times during the day. In addition to that, do not forget to floss your teeth regularly as well.

· Limit the consumption of food items and beverages, which might affect the appearance of your teeth. These include wine, coffee, and tea.

· Avoid smoking. Tobacco can lead to discoloration of teeth.

Do Not Ignore Bridges And Crowns

People often do not realize that teeth whitening might not be effective for bridges and crowns. As a result, when they undergo the process of teeth whitening, their natural teeth look whiter than their synthetic teeth.

This actually makes you look worse. Not to mention, in some cases, teeth whitening can even damage crowns and bridges.

Not Following After Care Instructions

After you go through the process of teeth whitening, there are certain instructions that you will need to follow as well. You will have to wait for at least a day before you can consume food items like sodas and sports drinks.  

Failure to do so will ruin the appearance of your teeth. In addition to that, it might also lead to tooth damage.

Not Consulting A Dentist

There are certain people who assume that over the counter teeth whitening products will give them the results they are looking for. They avoid going to the dentist and instead rely on these products. However, this can be risky.

It can lead to teeth damage. In addition to that, teeth whitening will not be effective if your teeth are discolored because of decay. This is why it is best to visit the dentists when you need to get your teeth whitened. Get in touch with Dentist Bethesda.
