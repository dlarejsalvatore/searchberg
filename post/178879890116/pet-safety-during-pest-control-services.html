Pet Safety During Pest Control Services

When a house is being treated for pest control, people are extremely vigilant about their kids being at a safe distance. However, the same level of care is not usually observed when it comes to the safety of pets.

In their frenzy to schedule pest treatment, people sometimes even forget to mention to their exterminator that they have pets.

Fortunately, professional pest control companies use pet-friendly control options. Still, there will be chemicals involved in the process that are designed to destroy uninvited invaders. These chemicals can be harmful for pets if they are inhaled, ingested or absorbed.

Here are a few measures that can be taken to ensure the safety of our furry, feathery and scaly friends during pest control treatment.

Communicate With Your Exterminator

Although good pest control companies will ask their clients beforehand if they have pets, not all companies do.

Make sure that your extermination company is aware that you have pets. Furthermore, you should tell your exterminator what types of pets you have and what areas in your home are most frequented by them.

Contain Your Pets

Pets such as cats and dogs are curious by nature. Their instincts tell them to investigate any new sound or smell.

However, when a home is being treated for pest control, curiosity can be a dangerous thing. Moreover, cats and dogs rely upon their noses to explore everything. If they are left in an area that is being treated for pests, it’s possible they might inhale, ingest or absorb the pesticide.

During pest control treatment, it’s advisable that you keep your four-legged friends in a kennel, a garage or in an area that is not being treated.

Birds, Fish and Other Pets

When it comes to pets such as birds, fish, hamsters and reptiles, special care needs to be observed. Tanks and cages should be covered up by blankets in such a way that mist and droplets formed by spraying pesticide cannot enter. Your veterinarian will be able to advise you in more detail on the precautions you should take with small pets during pest extermination.

What a Bed Bug Infestation Looks Like

Bed bugs—these reddish, tiny, wingless insects, can wreak quite the havoc.

According to the United States Environmental Protection Agency, bed bugs aren’t connected to any harmful diseases, but can have some major physical, mental and economic consequences.

Their bites can cause severe allergic reactions which can further lead to secondary skin infections, such as impetigo and lymphangitis. People living in bed bug infested homes have reported to be suffering from insomnia, anxiety and other mental health problems.

Bed bugs can be tricky to detect, especially for an untrained eye. You might not be able see the signs of the presence of bed bugs in your house until it’s too late. 

Here are some ways to help you figure out if you have a bed bug problem at your hands.

Itchy Red Bites

You might get bitten by a bed bug, even before you see one in your house. Bed bug bites are flat angry looking red welts, usually in small clusters or zigzag lines. These bites aren’t known to transmit any harmful diseases. However, they can cause irritation. If you scratch them, they might lead to a skin infection.

Bed Big Shells

Bed bugs grow in size as they feed on human or animal blood. This causes them to shed their outer shells; the process is known as molting. There are a total of five stages in the life span of a bed bug, and it molts each time as it goes through a phase. In an infested house, you’ll find these empty shells everywhere.

Blood Stains

If you have a bed bug infestation, you might find unexplained blood stains on your pillow cases, bed sheets, walls, furniture and clothes. These stains can be because of bed bug bites, but most of them are fecal spots. As bed bugs feed on blood, their fecal spotting tends to look like blood stains. 

Unpleasant Odor

You’re house might start getting an unpleasant smell to it if there are bed bugs present. A big group of bed bugs releases a large amount of pheromones, which can make your living room smell like a dirty locker room.

If you’ve noticed any of these signs in your house, it’s time to contact a bed bug exterminator service.

Pests That Can Wreak Havoc in Homes during winters

What comes to your mind when you think about winter? Usually people associate this season with Christmas and New Years. Naturally, pest infestations aren’t even the last things on their minds.

Pests and winters

Pests behave differently when the temperatures drop. Some of them hibernate during this time of the year while others look for a place where they can stay warm and have access to food.

Since this piece talks about winters, therefore the focus will be on the latter. Let’s take a look at some pests that you might see in your home during winters:

Norway Rats

These pests like to build nests in debris and basements and can eat through anything. This includes lead pipes as well as plastic. Like all other pests, their goal is to stay at a place where they have food and water.

The problem with them is that they can cause several health problems, which include jaundice and fever. What makes them especially troublesome is the fact that they can easily squeeze through tiny gaps.

You will know you have this problem if you notice gnaw marks on furniture and hear squeaking sounds coming from some parts of your house.  

Raccoons

Racoons enter your home through the attic or the chimney. What makes them dangerous is the fact that their bite carries the rabies virus.

House Mice

This is the most common rodent in the United States and is usually found in basements and attics. Like the Norway rats, these can gnaw through anything and cause significant property damage.

The worst part is that they can bite through wires, causing electric shorts and fires. They can also contaminate food and cause diseases.

Spiders

There’s something about spiders that just creeps you out doesn’t it? This is especially true in case of brown recluse spiders that spin webs in attics, closets and the basement. Their bite is venomous and can be really painful.

Cockroaches

Only German cockroaches can beat spiders in terms of creepiness. German cockroaches are commonly found in smaller areas where they have access to food and moisture. They can spread bacteria and pathogens, contaminating anything that they come into contact with.

Types of Fabric Pests You Should Know About

Everyone has that one item of clothing that they absolutely love. But what if one day you discover a tiny hole in that piece of clothing? To say that you would be upset may be an understatement.

Fabric pests are a nuisance and unfortunately, a common problem in the United States that becomes especially common during the fall.

What’s even worse is that there are different species of fabric pests and all of them can cause serious damage to your clothing and other fabrics. Take a look at the different types of pests that you should know about:

Carpet Beetles

These are very small and oval shaped. In addition to that, they also have knobbed antennae. Some of them also have colorful hair and scales on their bodies.

When present outdoors, these beetles feed on pollen. However, when they are indoors, they prefer to feed on unprotected clothing, especially silk.

In addition to that, the beetles also feed on accumulated pet hair and feathers. Therefore, if you have pets, these pests can be a cause of worry.

There are different species of carpet beetles. These include varied, black and furniture carpet beetles. All of these are different from each other in terms of appearance. Black beetles are especially dangerous since they can damage leather, silk, and carpets.

Varied carpet beetles too are dangerous since they don’t just damage clothing. They also cause damage to food products like cereal.

Clothes Moth

The first thing you need to know is that there are two species of clothes moths: case making cloth moths and webbing clothes moths.

Webbing clothes moths spin silk-like webbing on the fabric. It feeds under that webbing. It goes through several molts before pupating into an adult. These pests are around 8–10 mm long and are golden in color.

They can damage animal products, which include wool, skins, fur, and carpets. In addition to that, it’s not easy to detect their larvae.

Casemaking moths, on the other hand, live in a tubular case, which is made up of silk, and fibers. The larva drags the case around with it while it feeds. If it’s separated from the case, it will die. They can cause serious damage to woolen felts and upholstered furniture.

Are Bed Bugs Bugging You? Here’s how to see the last of them!

Bed bugs are more dangerous than you can imagine. They can cause allergies and infection, and the irritation from their bites can lead to insomnia.

These apple-seed sized critters come out at night to feed on human blood. Once they infest a home, it can get very difficult to get rid of them; however, they can be removed.

Here is what you should do if you discover bed bugs in your bed:

Reduce the Clutter

The first step towards removing bed bugs is to remove all personal items from the room, which includes clothes, blankets, electronics, etc. Remove anything that doesn’t stay in the room permanently.

However, be careful when removing these items. If these items are infested as well, the bed bugs will get transferred to other areas. A good idea, therefore, is to store these items in a plastic bag.

Launder Linen and Clothes

You cannot treat your bed linen and clothes with insecticide, so the best way to get rid of the bed bug from them is by laundering them.

Launder them with hot water and then heat them in the dryer for several minutes. This will kill all the bed bugs present in them.

Dismantle the Bed

Your mattress and the bed frame are probably the places where most of the bed bugs are hiding.

So, you must dismantle the bed frame in order to expose all the possible hiding spots. When you are cleaning and applying insecticides, it is necessary to have access to these hiding spots so that they are removed effectively.  You may also need to use a flashlight, as bed bugs are small and dark, can be extremely hard to detect.

Pull Furniture Away from the Wall

Pull all the furniture away from the walls and inspect if there are any bed bugs hiding. You should also pull out the dressing drawers, as they can be a convenient place for them to hide.

Vacuum

The next thing to do is to thoroughly vacuum your room. Ensure that you are dislodging the eggs and cleaning them using your vacuum.

You may need to need vacuum attachments to reach for those small crevices and cracks. The better you clean your furniture, the better the insecticide will work later on.

Once you are finished vacuuming the room, you should dispose of the bag after sealing it.

Pest Control

To apply the pesticide, you will need to hire a pest control service. Your ordinary bug spray won’t be effective against bed bugs and you probably aren’t certified to use advanced insecticides provides. This is why it is better to leave it to the professionals.

A proper pest control service will ensure that your room is completely rid of bed bugs.

5 Common Pest Control Myths You Need to Stop Believing

With the amount of information on the internet about pests and pest control, it is quite easy to fall victim to believing some of the most common myths surrounding the industry.

Some of these myths may be more harmful than you believe, and may even cause your pest problem to get worse instead of getting better.

So, before choosing to believe everything you find online, you may want to look into these common myths about pest control to avoid:

Myth#1: I only have a pest problem if I see the critters

A lot of pests like termites, carpenter ants and bed bugs are experts at hiding in wooden structures and between the folds of your sheets. Just because you don’t see them doesn’t mean they aren’t there!

At any given time, there may be more than 100 different species of bugs living inside your home. Believing this myth could lead to the infestation steadily increasing until they end up doing massive damage to your house. Larger nests are also more difficult to exterminate completely.

Here are the most common signs to look out for to know whether your house is suffering from a termite infestation.

Myth#2: Ultrasonic repellants are effective

It may sound like a miracle invention: imagine a device that could potentially eradicate bugs with the use of ultrasonic sound, below our threshold of hearing. But there has been little to no scientific backing for this method of pest control, and any pest extermination company claiming to offer this service is merely trying to fool you.

Myth#3: Clean houses do not have pests

Even the cleanest of houses has the potential of getting infested with pests. As long as there is a food source in the house—which for bugs like bed bugs is human and animal blood, and for rodents and cockroaches is food—there is always a possibility of infestation.

It is, however, true that a dirty house may have a higher possibility of pest infestations, as cluttered spaces give them more space to hide and more available food sources.

Myth#4: Your cat can get rid of your rodent problem

Most people who own domestic cats believe that the cat will take care of any and all rodent that may have found their way into your house. This assumption is false, as most well-fed cats lose the motivation to hunt.

While they may run around the house to catch a rodent, they are more likely to do so for play.

Myth#5: You can get rid of all pest infestations yourself

You cannot use the same method of pest control on every kind of pest. There are specific chemicals and insecticides that work on specific pests, and others that work better on others. While you may be able to get rid of smaller infestations on your own, when the problem gets too large to handle, it is best to call in a professional pest extermination service to effectively take care of your problem for you.

Pests That Are Likely to Invade Your Home This Summer

Summer can be very well considered as the season of insects. It is the time when most insects are either breeding, or waking up from hibernation in order to look for food and water sources.

What are some of the insects that are likely going to infest your home?

However here is a list of most common ones that infest homes during summer:

Mosquitoes

Mosquitoes may not sound like serious problems, but they are the deadliest insects on the planet. They are found in abundance throughout summer. The thing that attracts them the most is standing water, as that is where they lay their eggs.

The key to preventing mosquito infestations is breaking the reproduction cycle, by removing every source of stagnant water and exterminating the adults.

Ants

Ants usually hibernate during the winter and are all set for a full-scale infestation during summer. They look for sources of food and a place where they can set up a new colony and start saving reserves for the next season.

The best way to steer clear of an ant infestation is to clean up properly after every meal and have not have any food or water sources outside the house.

Termites

Summer is the nesting season for termites and they grow in numbers during this period. The queen can lay 30,000 eggs in a single day, which shows how quickly termites can grow.

Termites can cause thousands of dollars of damage in property, which is why you should consider taking action immediately at the first sign of infestation.

Flies

Flies usually infest homes during the summer to escape the heat outdoors. Summer is also the season in which they reproduce, which is why you will have a big problem once they infest your home. They will remain in your home until fall, unless you have a pest treatment.

Fleas

If you have pets in your home, you may encounter a flea problem. You may constantly find your pets scratching and biting themselves, especially after they come from outside.  

You can buy a Seresto collar to protect your pets from fleas; however, if the problem is a little too severe, then you may need a pest treatment in order to completely get rid of them.

Ticks

Unlike fleas, ticks are much more visible and are capable of spreading diseases. Ticks can be considered a summer pest simply because people and animals spend more time outside, which is where they usually thrive and attack.

Ticks also feed on rodents, so if your area has a high numbers of rodents, chances are you will likely have high number of ticks in your area.

5 Ways To Prevent A Pest Infestation

Dealing with a pest infestation can be quite challenging and expensive for homeowners. Back in 2015, pest infestation resulted in a loss of $13.2 billion for businesses in the United States.

Therefore, it is best to prevent this problem from occurring in the first place. Here is a look at some ways you can prevent a pest infestation:

Keep Your Eyes Open

Pests tend to come out in your absence however; they always leave behind a trail. Regularly inspect the interiors and the exteriors of your home. The ideal method of avoiding pests is to prevent them from entering your home in the first place.

Bathrooms are the most common entry point for most rats and cockroaches. Another area of your house, where these pests can make a home, is your kitchen. It provides access to a food source.

Installation Of Screens Over Chimneys

There are openings in certain areas of your home, which cannot be filled. Your chimney is one of them. Pests can make their way inside your home through openings in the chimney vent as well.

Another common problem that homeowners who ignore their chimneys face is a bat infestation. In order to prevent this from happening, you can install screens over chimneys.

Proper Food Storage

Pests are always looking for a source of food. They can find their way to the area where you store your food supplies. You can prevent this from happening by storing your food in a locked container.

Avoid placing food in cardboards since pests can easily enter them. Instead, place all open food either in plastic bags or in boxes, which have seals. Flies and ants are attracted to fruits because of which it is best to keep the fruits in the refrigerator.

Maintain Cleanliness In The House

Pest infestations are more common in households, which are not clean. They thrive in such environments since they can feed on the residues and crumbs that they find on the floor.

Clean the floor immediately in case of a spill. After you are done using the dishwasher, make sure that you do not leave the dishes to soak.

Along with food, pests also need water in order to survive. Install gutters in your home so that water can flow out of your home. Check all of the appliances in your home for any kind of leaks. In case you detect any kind of issues with these appliances, get them fixed immediately.

Seek Professional Help

One way you can make sure that your home is free from pests is by seeking help from professionals. They can conduct an inspection of your home prevent pests from causing damage to your home.
