Small Business Grants Program

Facebook has provided $100M in cash grants and ad credits to help small businesses across over 30 countries through our Small Business Grants Program. With our application period now closed across all countries, grant applicants can come back here for updates and answers to common questions.

The Small Business Grants Program has concluded it’s application period and will no longer be accepting new applications.

Facebook and it’s partners have finished accepting grant applications globally. If you have any questions, please refer to our FAQ below or reach out to your local application vendor.

Countries supported by grants program

Who can I speak to if I have more questions?

Unfortunately, we are unable to offer live support to recipients of a Facebook Small Business grant. We invite you to visit the Facebook Help Center or the Instagram Help Center if additional support is required. If you have questions on the status of your application, please reach out to the partner who is administering your application in your area.

How will my business receive the ad credit?

Recipients of a Facebook Small Business grant who opted in to receive the ad credit and completed the necessary steps, including providing a valid Facebook or Instagram account and a valid payment method on their account will have the ad credit automatically deposited into the provided ad account. In order for the ad credit to be deposited, the ad account must be active and have a valid payment method attached. Instructions and reminders for the required actions were sent by email to all grant recipients who opted in to receive the ad credit. Please check your spam folder if you are still missing these emails.

Why is a valid payment method required for the ad credit?

All Facebook ad accounts require a valid payment method, which will be used for charges if the ad credit runs out, but the business decides to continue running ads (see Ad Payment Methods)

How can businesses utilize the ad credit?

The ad credit can be utilized for any future ad spend across any of our advertising surfaces, including Facebook and Instagram. It cannot be used for ad spend that was accrued before the ad credit was applied, nor can it be used in any way that would violate Facebook's Advertising Policies or the terms of the Facebook Small Business Grant Program. As a reminder, the funds and ad credit from the grant award cannot be used to run political ads on our advertising surfaces. To learn more about political ads, please visit the Facebook Help Center.

How long will businesses have to spend the ad credit? Will extensions be granted?

The expiration date of the ad credit is listed in Ads Manager. Once the ad credit expires, any advertising costs incurred will be charged to the primary payment method associated with the ad account.

Unfortunately, extensions to the expiration date cannot be granted. Upon expiration, any unused portion of the ad credit will forfeited.

Why did the business receive the cash grant before the ad credit?

Our grants administrator needs to first inform us who is eligible to receive an ad credit. They do this after they distribute the cash grant and accordingly, there is a delay from the time the cash grant is paid to when the ad credit can be deposited.

In some instances, there may be some other issue preventing us from successfully depositing the ad credit. Common reasons, and instructions to resolve these issues are listed within this FAQ.

I was informed that I received a grant award. Why haven't I received my ad credit?

There could be a number of reasons why a Facebook Small Business Grant recipient has not received the ad credit. The ad credit was optional and grant recipients who opted in to receiving ad credits are required to complete additional steps, including providing a valid Facebook or Instagram account. Applicants who did not opt in or who did not complete the necessary steps such as providing valid Facebook or Instagram account details by the stated program deadlines are ineligible to receive ad credits.

If a grant recipient properly opted in to receive an ad credit, there could be a number of reasons why the deposit was unsuccessful. In each of these cases, the grant recipient has been sent an email with detailed instructions on how to resolve the issue. Is it possible to exchange ad credits for cash (or cash for ad credit)?

No. The cash and ad credit amounts are fixed.

Can I transfer the ad credit to another party or a different ad account?

No. The ad credit cannot be transferred under any circumstances.
